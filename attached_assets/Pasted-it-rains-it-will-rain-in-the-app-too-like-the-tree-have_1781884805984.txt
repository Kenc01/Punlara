NEW: Farmer Marketplace
Phase 2 of Punlara transforms it from one farm into a platform. Other fruit farmers in Zamboanga Peninsula — and eventually across Mindanao — can list their trees on Punlara. Think of it as the Airbnb of fruit trees.
A farmer in Zamboanga Sibugay with 50 rambutan trees signs up. They list their trees, set their price, and Punlara handles everything: the customer adoption, the digital certificate, the update schedule, the delivery coordination. Punlara takes a platform commission — 15 to 20% — while the farmer keeps the rest. This is a direct, fair income with no middlemen, which ties directly into SDG 8. The farmer gets more money. The customer gets more tree variety. Punlara scales without needing to own more land.

NEW: Blockchain Ownership Certificate
When you adopt a tree on Punlara, you receive a digital certificate — but not just a PDF. It is a blockchain-verified token that permanently records your ownership on-chain. This means:
Your certificate cannot be faked or duplicated. The tree's entire history — planting date, farm location, every update, every harvest — is recorded on the blockchain permanently. If you gift a tree to someone, the ownership transfer is recorded on-chain too. For corporations doing CSR tree adoption, this gives them a verifiable, auditable sustainability report they can show investors.
This feature directly hits the PSC coverage criteria — Web3 and Blockchain are explicitly listed. It also opens Punlara to carbon credit verification, where every tree's carbon offset is recorded on-chain and can be sold or claimed.

NEW: IoT Sensors — The Tech Behind the Life
The gamified experience is powered by real hardware on the farm:
Small IoT soil sensors are planted near every tree cluster, measuring soil moisture, temperature, and pH levels in real time. A weather station on the farm roof tracks rainfall, humidity, and sunlight hours. All this data feeds into the Punlara app via a low-cost IoT gateway — even a LoRaWAN module works in areas with low mobile signal. The app reads this data and drives the tree animations. Rain on the sensor means rain on the screen. Dry soil means the tree looks tired. This is not simulated — it is real farm data making your digital tree come alive.
