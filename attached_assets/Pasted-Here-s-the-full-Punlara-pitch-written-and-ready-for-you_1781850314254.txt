---

**THE PROBLEM**

Urban Filipinos buy fruit from supermarkets with no idea where it came from, how it was grown, or whether it was chemically treated. Small fruit farmers in Mindanao earn very little because they sell through multiple middlemen who take most of the profit. There is no direct connection between the person eating the fruit and the person growing it.

---

**THE SOLUTION — PUNLARA**

Punlara is a Philippine fruit tree adoption platform. The name comes from the Filipino word *punla* — seedling — because every customer starts something real and living.

Here is how it works. A customer visits Punlara, browses real fruit trees on a partner farm in Mindanao, and adopts one. They pay an annual fee, name their tree, and receive a Digital Adoption Certificate. A farmer plants or assigns that specific tree in the customer's name, tags it with a QR code, and sends monthly photo and video updates throughout the growing season. When harvest time comes, the customer's fruit is packed and delivered directly to their door — fresh, ripe, and from *their* tree.

---

**THE EXPERIENCE**

Imagine gifting your mother a mangosteen tree for her birthday. She names it. Every month she gets a video from the farm showing her tree growing. Then one day a box arrives at her door with mangosteen from her own tree, with a note that says "From your tree, with love." That is Punlara.

---

**WHY THE PHILIPPINES, WHY NOW**

This model already works globally. CrowdFarming in Europe and Rent-a-Tree in India have proven that urban consumers will pay a premium to own something real and traceable. Nobody has done this in the Philippines yet. We are the first mover in a country that is both the source of world-class tropical fruit and home to millions of urban consumers hungry for authentic, fresh food experiences.

---

**THE MARKET**

Our primary customers are urban professionals in Metro Manila and key cities aged 25 to 45 who care about food quality. Our secondary market is corporate gifting — companies looking for meaningful, eco-friendly gifts for employees and clients. Our third market is OFWs who want to send something deeply Filipino and personal to their families back home.

---

**THE BUSINESS MODEL**

Customers pay an annual tree adoption fee ranging from ₱1,500 for a Seedling tier up to ₱4,500 for a Premium tier with a mature tree and multiple harvests. Delivery is charged separately. We also offer Gift Tree packages for birthdays, anniversaries, and corporate events. Our gross margin per customer is approximately 60% after farm and delivery costs.

In Year 1 we target 300 adopters generating ₱750,000 in revenue. By Year 3 we project 2,000 adopters and ₱6,000,000 in annual revenue.

---

**THE TECHNOLOGY**

Every adopted tree has a unique QR code tag on the farm. Customers scan it to see their tree's live profile — photos, growth updates, harvest status, and delivery tracking. The Punlara web app handles adoption, certificate generation, dashboard updates, and payments via GCash and Maya.

---

**OUR UNFAIR ADVANTAGE**

We are not starting from zero. Our founding team has direct access to an existing fruit farm and supply chain in Central Luzon with established harvesting operations, workers, and delivery trucks. We are not building a farm — we are building a brand and technology layer on top of one that already works.

---

**THE IMPACT**

Punlara directly addresses three UN Sustainable Development Goals. SDG 2 — Zero Hunger — by connecting consumers to fresh traceable food. SDG 8 — Decent Work — by giving small farmers a fairer income with no middlemen. SDG 15 — Life on Land — through tree planting and sustainable farming practices. Every tree adopted is a tree planted. Every harvest delivered is a farmer paid fairly.
